Extinction Party

A satirical take on animal extinction awareness, browse through to learn how ten distinct animals went extinct.

Created in conjunction with four other team members, I was responsible for all interactions on this website. I also contributed heavily to the CSS and overall theme of the website. During group meetings, I lead discussion, delegated work, and helped her teammates complete their portions.

This website was made for ART 101 at UCSC taught by Wes Modes during Spring 2022.

The Last Heart

In Nequam Mountain, the undead seeks to rise again. Warriors from the past have left items to aid you quest, but every boon has its cost. Will you be able to defeat the enemy within and save your people?

For this project, we wanted to create an extremely unforgiving hack-and-slash RPG. I was responsible for coordinating all group members by creating a development plan, running meetings, and keeping track of tasks to complete. I also contributed to level design, programming, story, and other assorted art.

This game was made using the free version of Construct3 for CMPM 80k at UCSC taught by Tad Leckman and Noah Wardrip-Fruin during Winter 2022.

The World Moves On

What do you do after something unbearable happens to someone you love? How can you help if they're stuck in the past?

In this interactive narrative, I wanted to look at loss and depression from an outside perspective. I worked in monotone to set the tone and used text placement and font to differentiate between each voice.

This project was made using an engine created by Adam Smith for CMPM 35 at UCSC in Fall 2021.

The Unfortunate Tail of Fancy the Cat

A story about a cat that always ends horribly.

In this branching narrative, I played with a contrast between a silly visual aesthetic and a sad and dark topic. I wanted to explore surface level success and an unhealthy obsession with work, both of which are topics that I'm very familiar with.

This project was made using an engine created by Adam Smith for CMPM 35 at UCSC in Fall 2021.
